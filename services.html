Roofing Services Built for Atlantic Canada

Every material we stock, every technique we use, and every system we install is chosen specifically for Maritime weather. Here is what we do and how we do it.

Residential Roofing

Your home is the biggest investment you will ever make, and the roof is what keeps everything underneath it dry, warm, and structurally sound. A failing roof does not just leak — it rots decking, grows mold in insulation, destroys drywall, and tanks your resale value. We replace and repair residential roofs across HRM with materials and methods designed for decades of Maritime service.

Asphalt Shingles

The most popular residential roofing material in Nova Scotia for good reason. Modern architectural shingles offer a dimensional look that mimics wood shake or slate at a fraction of the cost. We install IKO Dynasty, CertainTeed Landmark, and GAF Timberline HDZ — all rated for 210 km/h wind uplift, which matters when November rolls around. A full asphalt re-shingle on a typical Halifax bungalow takes our crew two to three days and comes with a 15-year workmanship warranty on top of the manufacturer's 50-year limited material coverage.

Metal Roofing

Standing seam and snap-lock metal panels are increasingly popular across Nova Scotia, and for good reason. Metal sheds snow loads that would crush asphalt, resists salt air corrosion when properly coated, and lasts 40 to 60 years with virtually zero maintenance. We install 26-gauge galvalume and 24-gauge steel panels with concealed fastener systems that eliminate the leak points common in exposed-screw agricultural panels. Metal costs more upfront but pencils out over 20 years when you factor in the re-shingle you will not need to do.

Cedar Shake

For heritage homes and character properties in Halifax's south end, nothing matches the look of hand-split cedar shake. We source Western Red Cedar from BC mills and install over a ventilated skip-sheathing system that lets each shake breathe and dry properly in our humid climate. Cedar requires more maintenance than asphalt — expect to treat it every five to seven years — but a well-maintained cedar roof lasts 30 years and adds genuine curb appeal that asphalt simply cannot replicate.

Flat and Low-Slope Residential

Townhouses, row houses, and modern flat-roof designs need membrane systems, not shingles. We install modified bitumen (torch-on) and TPO membranes on residential flat roofs with proper tapered insulation to ensure positive drainage. Standing water is the number one killer of flat roofs, and most of the failed residential flat roofs we tear off were installed without any slope — just membrane laid over flat decking. We fix that every time.

TPO Single-Ply Membrane

Thermoplastic polyolefin is the fastest-growing commercial roofing system in North America. TPO membranes are heat-welded at every seam, creating a monolithic waterproof barrier with no adhesive to fail. We install Carlisle, Firestone, and Johns Manville TPO systems in 60-mil and 80-mil thicknesses, mechanically attached or fully adhered depending on wind exposure and building height. TPO reflects UV radiation, reducing cooling costs in summer — though in Nova Scotia, the real benefit is chemical resistance to the salt and industrial pollutants that eat through lesser membranes.

EPDM Rubber Roofing

Ethylene propylene diene monomer rubber has been the workhorse of commercial flat roofing for decades. It is flexible at minus-40, handles thermal cycling without cracking, and has a proven track record spanning 40 years. We install EPDM in ballasted, mechanically attached, and fully adhered configurations. For buildings where foot traffic is expected — rooftop HVAC access, for example — we overlay EPDM with walkway pads to prevent puncture damage. Our EPDM installations include fully reinforced corners and penetration details that are the first place cheap installations fail.

Built-Up Roofing (BUR)

Multiple alternating layers of bitumen and reinforcing fabric create the most redundant waterproofing system available. BUR has been protecting commercial buildings for over a century because the multi-ply approach means a single puncture does not compromise the whole system. We install three-ply and four-ply BUR systems with gravel or mineral cap sheet surfacing, depending on aesthetics and foot traffic requirements. BUR is heavier than single-ply systems, so we always verify structural capacity before specifying it.

Inspections & Preventive Maintenance

The cheapest roof repair is the one you never need. Our preventive maintenance program catches small issues — cracked caulking, lifted flashing, clogged drains — before they become expensive emergencies. A $200 maintenance visit today prevents a $5,000 repair next winter.

Seasonal Inspection Program

Our maintenance customers receive two comprehensive inspections per year: one in spring (to assess winter damage and clear debris) and one in fall (to prepare for snow loads and freeze-thaw cycles). Each inspection covers shingle condition, flashing integrity, valley and hip seals, vent boot condition, gutter and downspout flow, soffit ventilation, and attic moisture levels. You receive a written report with photos and a prioritized list of any repairs needed.

Gutter and Drainage Maintenance

Clogged gutters cause more roof damage than most homeowners realize. When water backs up behind ice dams or overflows into fascia boards, it rots the roof edge from the outside in. Our maintenance program includes full gutter cleaning, downspout flushing, and gutter guard inspection. For commercial flat roofs, we clear interior drains, scuppers, and overflow outlets to ensure positive drainage before every freeze season.

Warranty Protection

Our inspection reports serve as proof that your roof has been professionally maintained, protecting your warranty coverage for the full term. We keep digital records of every inspection and repair for the life of your roof, accessible anytime you need them for warranty claims, insurance, or property sales.
