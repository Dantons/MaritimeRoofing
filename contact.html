Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most from homeowners and property managers.

How much does a new roof cost in Halifax?

A complete residential roof replacement in Halifax typically ranges from $8,000 to $18,000 for a standard single-family home, depending on size, pitch, material choice, and the number of layers that need to be removed. Steep roofs, dormers, and multiple penetrations (skylights, chimneys, vents) add complexity and cost. We provide detailed written estimates broken down by line item so you know exactly what you are paying for. Our estimates are valid for 60 days and the price we quote is the price you pay — no surprise extras.

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most residential roof replacements take two to four days from tear-off to final cleanup. A simple single-layer removal on a straightforward gable roof can be done in two days. Complex roofs with multiple layers, extensive decking repair, or difficult access may take up to a week. Weather delays are always possible in Nova Scotia — we monitor forecasts closely and will not start a tear-off if rain is expected within 48 hours. Your interior is never exposed overnight; we tarp and seal at the end of every work day.

Do you handle the insurance claim?

We do not file the claim for you — that is between you and your insurer — but we do everything else. We document the damage with photos and written descriptions, prepare a detailed scope of work in the format your insurer expects, meet with the adjuster on site, and respond to any supplemental information requests. About 80% of our insurance-related jobs are approved on first submission, and we have a 95% success rate on claims we support. We never inflate damage or recommend unnecessary work to increase a claim.

What is the best roofing material for Nova Scotia weather?

For most residential applications, architectural asphalt shingles offer the best balance of durability, cost, and performance in our climate. Look for shingles rated to 210 km/h wind uplift and Class 4 impact resistance. Metal roofing is an excellent choice if you plan to stay in your home long term — it handles snow loads better than shingles and can last 50-plus years. We do not recommend three-tab shingles anymore; the price difference versus architectural is small but the performance gap is enormous, especially in high-wind areas along the coast.

Can you work on my roof in winter?

Yes, with some limitations. Asphalt shingles should ideally be installed above 5 degrees Celsius because the adhesive strips need warmth to seal properly. Below that temperature, we use hand-sealing techniques and specialized cold-weather adhesives, but installation takes longer and costs slightly more. Metal roofing can be installed year-round. Emergency repairs and tarping happen regardless of temperature or conditions — that is the whole point of emergency service. If your project can wait until spring without risk of further damage, we will tell you so.
